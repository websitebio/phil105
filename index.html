Module 1 - Arguments

This course covers the concept of arguments and how to identify and analyze them. It also covers the concepts of statements, non-arguments, vagueness, ambiguity, generalizations, biases, and dispositions. Students should be able to identify arguments and non-arguments, identify statements and non-statements, resolve vagueness and ambiguity, identify different types of generalizations, identify confirmation bias, reflect on their own open-mindedness, and understand the influence of their own worldview. They should also be able to define terms such as argument, premise, conclusion, premise indicator, conclusion indicator, disposition, open-mindedness, psychological bias, confirmation bias, and worldview. The course also covers the process of evaluating arguments, including considering the evidence, context, and alternative explanations, as well as identifying fallacies in reasoning.
